Passive Sonar Systems rely on detecting sound waves from the surrounding environment, allowing submarines to gather information without revealing their location. In contrast, Active Sonar Systems emit sound pulses and analyze the echoes returning from underwater objects, providing precise situational awareness. Both types play crucial roles in naval operations, enhancing underwater detection, navigation, and engagement capabilities in varying operational scenarios.</p></p>
