The market is primarily divided into two types: organic scintillators, which typically use hydrocarbon-based compounds for neutron detection, offering advantages in sensitivity and fast response times; and inorganic scintillators, which incorporate crystalline structures like sodium iodide or lithium glass, known for their durability and high light output. Both types serve diverse applications in nuclear safety, medical imaging, and radiation monitoring, addressing specific detection requirements.</p></p>
