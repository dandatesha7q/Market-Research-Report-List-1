<p><p>Titanium dioxide (TiO2) is a widely used pigment renowned for its high opacity and reflective properties, making it ideal for optical white reflectors. The market for TiO2 is primarily divided into two production processes: the sulfate process and the chloride process. The sulfate process involves treating titanium ore with sulfuric acid, yielding lower-quality TiO2, while the chloride process uses titanium tetrachloride and is known for producing higher-purity and more efficient TiO2, favored in applications requiring superior optical performance.</p></p>
